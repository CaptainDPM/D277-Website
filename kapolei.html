What makes Hawaii so special?

Kapolei is a vibrant city located on the western coast of the island of Oahu, Hawaii. Situated about 20 miles west of Honolulu, Kapolei is often referred to as the "Second City" of Oahu due to its rapid growth and development. The city serves as a major residential and commercial hub, offering a wide range of amenities, recreational opportunities, and a high quality of life. With its modern infrastructure, planned communities, and proximity to beautiful beaches, Kapolei has become a popular destination for both residents and visitors alike.

Side note:

Kapolei is slowly becoming the new Honolulu! From the waterpark to the new mall, there's no reason not to go!


Kapolei is known for its master-planned communities, providing a mix of residential areas, shopping centers, office spaces, and recreational facilities. The city boasts a number of well-designed neighborhoods, offering a variety of housing options from single-family homes to apartments and townhouses. Residents enjoy easy access to a range of amenities, including shopping malls, restaurants, movie theaters, and community parks. The city's focus on sustainable development is evident in its commitment to renewable energy, green spaces, and pedestrian-friendly infrastructure.

One of the notable attractions in Kapolei is the Ko Olina Resort, a luxurious destination featuring world-class resorts, golf courses, and a picturesque marina. Visitors can indulge in upscale accommodations, fine dining, and relaxation by the beach. The resort is also home to Aulani, a Disney resort and spa that offers a unique blend of Hawaiian culture and Disney magic. Kapolei is also known for its shopping opportunities, with the Ka Makana Ali'i shopping center being a popular destination for retail therapy. For outdoor enthusiasts, nearby attractions include beautiful beaches like Ko Olina Lagoons and the stunning Kaena Point State Park, offering opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, hiking, and bird watching. Kapolei's combination of modern amenities, natural beauty, and a thriving community make it an appealing destination for both residents and visitors seeking a contemporary Hawaiian experience.
